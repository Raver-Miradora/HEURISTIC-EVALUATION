D – Determine the Goals

This evaluation aims to identify usability issues impacting operational efficiency, inventory accuracy, and user satisfaction within the Computer Laboratory Management System. We will assess how effectively staff can perform core tasks like equipment borrowing, inventory tracking, and damage reporting. The analysis will also evaluate user trust in system data, particularly for sensitive functions involving item availability and borrower records.

E – Explore the Questions

  1. Visibility of System Status
    • Does the design clearly communicate its state?
    • Is feedback presented quickly after user actions?
  2. Match Between System and the Real World
    • Will users be familiar with the terminology?
    • Do the design's controls follow real-world conventions?
  3. User Control and Freedom
    • Does the design allow users to go back a step in the process?
    • Are exit links easily discoverable?
    • Can users easily cancel an action?
    • Is Undo and Redo supported?
  4. Consistency and Standards
    • Does the design follow industry conventions?
    • Are visual treatments used consistently throughout the design?
  5. Error Prevention
    • Does the design prevent slips by using helpful constraints?
    • Does the design warn users before they perform risky actions
  6. Recognition Rather Than Recall
    • Does the design keep important information visible, so that users do not have to memorize it?
    • Does the design offer help in-context?
  7. Flexibility and Efficiency of Use
    • Does the design provide accelerators like keyboard shortcuts and touch gestures?
    • Is content and functionality personalized or customized for individual users?
  8. Aesthetic and Minimalist Design
    • Is the visual design and content focused on the essentials?
    • Have all distracting, unnecessary elements been removed?
  9. Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors
    • Does the design use traditional error message visuals, like bold, red text?
    • Does the design offer a solution that solves the error immediately?
  10. Help and Documentation
    • Is help documentation easy to search?
    • Is help provided in context right at the moment when the user requires it?

C - Choose the evaluation approach and methods.

For our evaluation approach, we employed heuristic evaluation using Jakob Nielsen’s 10 usability principles to systematically assess the Computer Laboratory Management System’s interface and functionality. Each team member conducted the evaluation individually to independently identify usability issues, ensuring unbiased and diverse perspectives. This method allowed us to scrutinize the system’s compliance with key usability heuristics. After completing individual assessments, we consolidated findings to prioritize issues.

I: Identify the practical issues.

Heuristic Evaluation Team Composition/Participants

The evaluation was conducted by a team of IT undergraduates from Partido State University, serving as trained heuristic evaluators. The team was structured with the following roles:

  1. Lead Evaluator - Coordinated the evaluation process and consolidated findings from all team members
  2. Heuristic Specialists (5 members) - Each focused on evaluating specific Nielsen heuristics to ensure comprehensive coverage
  3. Documentation Specialist - Recorded all identified usability issues with screenshots and detailed descriptions

D - Decide on the Ethical Issues

For our heuristic evaluation of the Computer Laboratory Management System, we established the following ethical guidelines:

  1. Confidentiality Agreement
    • All system screenshots and findings were anonymized to protect sensitive data
  2. Evaluation Integrity
    • Findings were documented objectively without fabrication or exaggeration
    • All identified issues were verified by at least two team members before reporting
  3. Professional Conduct
    • Testing used dummy data rather than real user information
  4. Attribution
    • Proper credit was given to Jakob Nielsen's framework

E - Evaluation, Analyze, Interpret and Present the data

For our evaluation process, we utilized the Nielsen Norman Group Heuristic Evaluation Workbook as our primary assessment tool. This comprehensive workbook provided structured fillable forms and detailed guiding questions aligned with Jakob Nielsen's 10 usability principles. Each evaluator systematically examined the Computer Laboratory Management System using these standardized templates, which helped maintain consistency across all assessments. The workbook's framework enabled us to methodically document observed issues, their corresponding heuristic violations and improvement recommendations.
